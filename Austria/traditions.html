Egg Pecking

An easter tradition in Austria called egg pecking or in Austrian eierpecken is a tradition where two people hold an egg and knock the ends of their eggs against each other once. The point of this tradition is to crack the other egg without cracking yours which then allows you to claim the other egg. If you strike too hard you both lose and if you strike too soft your opponent has an advantage, this tradition requires skill and speed control.

Christmas Traditions

Christmas markets in Austria are an old tradition which dates back to the middle ages. Christmas markets play a big role in the holiday season where locals gather in this festive atmosphere, the markets offer things like traditional crafts, foods and drinks including wine and gingerbread but also Christmas decorations. On Christmas eve Austrian families gather around the beautifully decorated Christmas tree to open Christmas presents whilst enjoying the time with singing carols.

Bonfire

Easter bonfires, known as "Osterfeuer," is an Austrian Easter tradition where you light up branches. On the night before Easter Sunday, communities come together to create a huge pile of branches with branches that have been collected throughout the year. Wooden boxes are also added to the bonfire to manage the fire and to make sure it burns steadily and safely, The bonfires can reach up to six meters. This tradition represents the welcoming back of the sun and the winter is going away.
