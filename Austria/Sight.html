A must see for everyone who likes music or just beautiful architecture in general is the Vienna State Opera. Established in 1869

The Kunsthistorisches Museum is a veritable gold mine for art lovers. It is also called the Museum of Art History and is teeming with amazing art treasures. It is located close to the Hofburg Palace.

Entering Schönbrunn Palace is like entering a fantasy world. In the seventeenth century, it was converted from a hunting lodge into a spectacular baroque house.

Schönbrunn Palace

It's difficult to comprehend its immense size with 1,440 rooms The State Apartments, the Hall of Mirrors, and the Great Gallery are a few of the most striking spaces. Beautiful frescoes, glistening chandeliers, and minute decorations abound throughout every room. However, the palace is more than just its apartments. The gardens, which feature exquisite fountains, statues, and well kept grass, are similarly magnificent. Imagine discovering hidden passageways, meandering through a flower filled maze, and discovering exquisite sculptures everywhere you look. On the grounds of the palace sits the oldest zoo in the world, Tiergarten Schönbrunn.

This opera house has gained worldwide recognition, and properly so. It presents a plethora of opera and ballet performances annually that entice music enthusiasts from across the world. However, even if you dont like operas, the structure is grasping. Its architecture is a work of art, and going on a guided tour will allow you to see some of the behind the scenes places and learn about its history. You may even witness a rehearsal in process while learning about its significance to Vienna's cultural history. Imagine strolling through the elegant halls, taking a seat on the stage where well known plays have been performed, and breathing in the history that permeates the space.

Vienna State Opera

Kunsthistorisches Museum

This museum is home to everything from Renaissance masterpieces to treasures from ancient Egypt. There are pieces by wellknown artists like Vermeer, caravaggio, and Raphael. The structure itself, with its opulent staircases and exquisitely furnished halls, is a piece of beauty. With its striking displays and breathtaking exhibitions, the Kunsthistorisches Museum has the power to captivate even those who aren't typically museum visitors. Imagine being mere inches away from hundreds of years of ancient paintings that each tell a historical tale.
